Cape Naturaliste

On the bottom left-hand corner of Australia there is a squarish sticky out bit. The top left corner is formed by Cape Naturaliste and the bottom left corner is formed by Cape Leeuwin. In between is the Margaret River region. This regions has to be in our top three in all of Australia. Have look at the images and you will see why.

Most of the coastline is pristine wilderness. Rocky granite headlands, forming bays of white sandy beaches. The untamed Indian Ocean swells roll in ceaselessly creating magic scenery and amazing surfing. The hilly coastal scrub is home to all manner of unique flora and fauna.

South of Cape Naturaliste are the Canel Rocks.

From above you can see where the name comes from.

Some close ups of the Canel Rocks scenery...

We spent two lunch breaks overlooking Smiths Beach. Watching the surfers and walkers.

Coming east from Cape Naturaliste is Bunker Bay.

Further east is Eagle Bay. Typical sandy beachs almost devoid of people. What is not to like?

Further east toward Geographe Bay is Gannet Rock.

Again south of Cape Naturaliste is Sugarloaf Rock is one of those places you can go day after day just sit and absorb the peace and wonder

Carnaby Cockatoo is one of the birds species Jenelle had been seaching for quite a while. Finally two got close enough to photograph.

Even rarier bird on her list is the Western Emu-wren. We spent over two hours one day walking the paths around the Cape Naturaliste lighthouse. We seen a few but they were too fast and too secretive to get anymore than a brown blur. John had got a good look at a few but not Jenelle. The next day, with renewed determination we went back to seach again. It wasn't easy but Jenelle did get a few good shots. Everybody was cock-a-hoop!

We took some interesting shots of other birds too.
